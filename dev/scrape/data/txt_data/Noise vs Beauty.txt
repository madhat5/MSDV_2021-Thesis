<a class="heading__t" href="#ethic">ethic</a></h3>


<div class="markdown"><ol>
<li><p>Bury your faeces and carry out your toilet paper. If the ground is too hard to bury your faeces, please carry it out with you and dispose of it in the campsite bins. Poop bags are available for free at De Pakhys. (A special note about this: <a class="internal" data-nid="1307745243" href="/climbing/south-africa/rocklands/area/1307745243">Tea Garden</a> has been closed primarily due to this problem. Animals such as baboons may eat human faeces and could contract diseases such as Tuberculosis and hepatitis, which could prove detrimental to the population)</p></li>
<li><p>Do not litter – carry everything you bring in with you back out with you and dispose of your litter in the bins at the campsite.</p></li>
<li><p>Stick to the allocated paths marked by cairns and as illustrated in the guidebook. Diverting from these paths causes far more erosion than is necessary and may cause the extinction of certain sensitive plants in the area.</p></li>
<li><p>No graffiti on rock surfaces.  (<a class="internal" data-nid="1307764077" href="/climbing/south-africa/rocklands/route/1307764077">Black Shadow</a> boulder has been closed to climbing due to graffiti)</p></li>
<li><p>No pof is allowed in <a class="internal" data-nid="12554869" href="/climbing/south-africa/rocklands">Rocklands</a>.  The resin damages the rock surfaces and this damage is irreversible.</p></li>
</ol>

<p>The complete disrespect of boulderers for the land on which they climb is a very serious and has become a very real threat to bouldering in <a class="internal" data-nid="12554869" href="/climbing/south-africa/rocklands">Rocklands</a>.</p>

<p>Rocklands bouldering is in peril. <a class="internal" data-nid="12554869" href="/climbing/south-africa/rocklands">Rocklands</a> does not belong to the climbers.</p>

<p>Our access is not a right, it’s a privilege and our treatment of the land is the difference between us being allowed to climb in <a class="internal" data-nid="12554869" href="/climbing/south-africa/rocklands">Rocklands</a> and us being banned from climbing in <a class="internal" data-nid="12554869" href="/climbing/south-africa/rocklands">Rocklands</a> altogether.</p>

<p>Irresponsible boulderers have caused this problem and boulderers are the only ones who can fix it.</p>

<p>Please respect the land you climb on in South Africa and adhere to the above mentioned rules.  Your privilege to climb in <a class="internal" data-nid="12554869" href="/climbing/south-africa/rocklands">Rocklands</a> depends on it.</p>
</div>
